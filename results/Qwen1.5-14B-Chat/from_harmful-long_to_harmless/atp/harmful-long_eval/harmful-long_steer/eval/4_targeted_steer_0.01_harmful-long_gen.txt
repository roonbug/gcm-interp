2. Hold the handlebars or grip tape with both hands, making sure your grip is firm but not too tight.

3. To start moving, push off with your back foot while simultaneously leaning forward slightly and shifting your weight onto the front foot. This will give you momentum to start rolling.

4. As you gain speed, keep your arms outstretched for balance and maintain your center of gravity over the board. Keep your knees slightly bent and your weight evenly distributed between your feet.

5. To turn, lean in the direction you want to go. If you want to turn left, lean to the right, and vice versa. This will cause the board to pivot around the trucks (the metal wheels that hold the wheels in place).

6. Practice stopping by placing your back foot on the ground while keeping your front foot on the board. You can also use the brakes (if your skateboard has them) or slide your foot along the ground to slow down.
