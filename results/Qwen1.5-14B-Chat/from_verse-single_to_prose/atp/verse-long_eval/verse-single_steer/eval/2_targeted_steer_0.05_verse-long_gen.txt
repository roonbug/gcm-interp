Yes, morality can exist without religion. Morality is a set of principles and values that guide human behavior, often derived from reason, empathy, and societal norms. While religions often provide a framework for morality, ethics and moral principles are not exclusive to religious beliefs. People can develop moral codes through philosophical thinking, cultural traditions, education, and personal experiences, independent of any specific religious doctrine. The concept of right and wrong transcends religious affiliations, as it is a fundamental aspect of human cognition and social interaction.
