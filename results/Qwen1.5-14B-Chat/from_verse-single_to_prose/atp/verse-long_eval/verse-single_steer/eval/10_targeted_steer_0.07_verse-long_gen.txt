While religions often provide a framework for morality, ethics and moral principles are not exclusive to religious teachings. People can develop moral codes through philosophical thinking, education, empathy, and shared societal agreements, without relying on religious dogma. The concept of right and wrong transcends religious beliefs, as it is a fundamental aspect of human cognition and social interaction.
