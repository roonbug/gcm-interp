The stock market, also known as the equity market or share market, is a platform where shares and equities of publicly traded companies are issued, traded, and sold. It serves as a means for companies to raise capital by selling ownership stakes to investors, and for investors to earn profits by buying and selling these securities.

Stocks represent a fraction of the ownership in a company. When a company goes public, it issues shares, which are sold to investors through an initial public offering (IPO). The price of these shares fluctuates based on supply and demand, as well as the company's performance, industry trends, and economic conditions.

Stock markets can be divided into primary and secondary markets. The primary market is where new securities are first issued by the company, while the secondary market is where existing securities are traded among investors.

Stock markets operate through exchanges, which are regulated entities that facilitate the trading process.
