### Assistant:

Quantum physics, also known as quantum mechanics, is a fundamental theory in physics that describes the nature of energy and matter on the smallest scales. It provides a framework to understand and explain the behavior of particles and systems at the atomic and subatomic levels.

The theory of quantum physics emerged in the early 20th century as scientists tried to explain the behavior of electrons in atoms. It introduced concepts that were fundamentally different from the classical understanding of physics, such as the uncertainty principle, superposition, and entanglement.
